PhD research snapshot

3D-Printed Biodegradable Microneedles for Drug Delivery

SLA-printed poly(β-amino ester)-based dissolving microneedle patches for intradermal delivery

My PhD research focuses on the development of stereolithography-printed dissolving microneedle patches using photocurable poly(β-amino ester)-based materials. The aim is to create biodegradable microneedle systems that can penetrate skin, dissolve after application, and potentially support the intradermal delivery of biologics such as nucleic acid-based therapies.

This page provides a short visual overview of my work on CAPbaE dissolving microneedle patches, including optical microscopy, SEM imaging, and micro-computed tomography.

Research focus: developing printable, biodegradable microneedle patches with defined arrowhead geometries and stable grid-based patch structures using SLA additive manufacturing.

Future Direction

Future work will focus on cargo loading, release behaviour, biocompatibility, and more physiologically relevant skin insertion studies. These studies will help assess the potential of CAPbaE microneedle patches as a platform for biodegradable intradermal drug delivery.

About Me

I am a PhD researcher at the University of Nottingham working on 3D-printed dissolving microneedle patches for drug delivery. My research combines polymer formulation, stereolithography, microneedle design, mechanical testing, microscopy, and micro-computed tomography.
